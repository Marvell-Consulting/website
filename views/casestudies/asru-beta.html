Building an exemplar licensing service

UK regulatory body

Background

In 2017, we replaced a legacy IT system for a body who licenses scientific research in the UK.

After making the system live in August 2019, we were re-commissioned to research, design and build improvements to the system.

The initial system allowed scientists to apply for and manage their licences online and allowed the regulator to grant or refused licences. The improvements requested included:

  • digitising the process used to assess licence applications
  • digitising compliance processes, such as annual reporting used to produce government statistics
  • allowing advanced search of project licences to support evidence-based policy-making
  • being able to view the history of a licence
  • designing a dashboard with performance data for managers

Improving a live service

As the service was live it was important we continued to maintain the core architecture and add new features in ways that weren’t disruptive to users.

Any issues raised in feedback were reviewed in weekly meetings and prioritised for fixing by the development team. In the meantime the research and design team explored how to incorporate new features into the working software. In total we carried out over 270 research sessions as we designed, tested and iterated the new features before building them into the live software.

Together with the regulator we agreed a robust communication strategy to keep licence-holders informed of changes to the system. This included weekly emails and presenting at industry conferences and events.

[The regulator] seem to have focused on customer service a lot more in the last two years, they listen to us and ask our views on things. The online service has been a major shift - the change has been expertly managed with lots of clear communications. Feedback from a research establishment

Results

User satisfaction has increased substantially. A survey of research establishments recently showed:

  • a rise in the percentage of users satisfied with the online service from 47% to 78% – this includes a rise in those very satisfied from 5% to 33%
  • a rise in the percentage of users who are confident in the regulator’s ability to provide an effective service from 77% to 85% – this includes a rise in those very confident from 15% to 46%

The service has been chosen as a government exemplar for complex digital transformations and features in a blog post for the services in government blog.
